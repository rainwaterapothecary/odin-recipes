I recently rediscovered this dish on a trip to Fresco, Texas.I remember it being one of the only dishes my mother ever prepared the right way.

These chickpea balls can come out a little grainy, but they are filling and delicious, especially with some green goddess sauce!

Ingredients

  • Dried chickpeas not from a can
  • Parsley, cilantro, dill
  • Onions, yellow or white
  • Garlic cloves, the fresher the better!
  • Kosher salt and pepper, to taste
  • Baking powder

Steps

  1. Soak dried chickpeas for 24 hours, baking soda helps to soften them. Drain well.
  2. Combine all ingredients in a food processor. Pulse a little at a time until the texture is like a coarse meal.
  3. Refridgerate for one hour or overnight. This will help form the balls better.
  4. Roll into balls like cookie dough.
  5. Fry slowly in medium-high heat until brown.
  6. Enjoy in a warm pita with your choice of toppings!
